YOLO Vehicle Detection: Resolution, Compression & GAN-Enhanced Evaluation

Comparative study of YOLOv8, YOLOv10, and YOLOv12 on Poribohon-BD (16 Bangladeshi vehicle classes) under resolution scaling, JPEG compression (Q50/Q75), and Real-ESRGAN restoration. Comprehensive analysis of real-world ITS degradation scenarios.

Python YOLOv8 YOLOv10 YOLOv12 Computer Vision Real-ESRGAN

Dataset: Poribohon-BD v2

9,058 images across 16 vehicle classes from Bangladesh — the most diverse and region-representative vehicle detection benchmark for South Asia. Classes include region-specific transport modes (CNG auto-rickshaw, easy-bike, leguna, rickshaw) not present in Western benchmarks (COCO, KITTI). All images manually annotated with Pascal VOC XML; human faces blurred for privacy. Data augmentation expanded training set to 14,400 samples (900 per class) via geometric and photometric transformations.

Methodology

Dataset: Poribohon-BD v2 (9,058 JPEG images, 16 vehicle classes, Pascal VOC XML annotations)

Preprocessing: Letterbox padding + aspect-ratio-preserving resize to 1024×1024 → augmentation (900 per class) → 14,400 total samples

Variants: (1) Resized to 64/128/256/512/1024px, (2) JPEG compressed (Q50/Q75), (3) GAN-enhanced with Real-ESRGAN

Models: YOLOv8m, YOLOv10m, YOLOv12l (identical hyperparameters for fair ablation)

Metrics: mAP@0.5 (IoU=0.5), mAP@0.5:0.95 (IoU 0.5–0.95, step 0.05)

Hardware: NVIDIA RTX 3080 (10GB), Intel i9, 64GB RAM

Key Findings

  • Resolution dominates. mAP@0.5 rises from ~60% at 64px to ~95% at 1024px across all models.
  • YOLOv8 is most stable. Anchor-free head + efficient backbone deliver best/most consistent results across all degradation scenarios.
  • YOLOv10 excels at low resolution. Efficiency-focused design extracts sufficient structural patterns even at 64px (60.29% vs YOLOv8's 60.05%).
  • YOLOv12 (attention-centric) is competitive. Strong at higher resolutions with compression/GAN, but degrades at low res due to reliance on rich feature representations.
  • Compression impact scales with resolution. At 1024px, Q50 loss is <1 point; at 64px, it's 3 points. Low-res images lack redundancy to absorb compression.
  • GAN helps selectively. At high resolution + compression, GAN restoration lifts mAP to 95.70% (best overall). At low res, hallucinated details confuse detector, hurting accuracy.
  • Perceptual fidelity ≠ detection accuracy. Images that look better to humans can degrade model performance; GANs optimize for visual plausibility, not machine understanding.

Ablation & Insights

Resolution ablation: Confirms mAP saturates around 512–1024px for Poribohon-BD. Moving from 256→512px yields +5–6 mAP points; 512→1024px yields +1–2 points. Practical threshold likely 512px for real-world intelligent transportation systems (ITS).

Compression ablation: Q50 vs Q75 shows diminishing returns; both degrade low-res severely. Q75 acceptable at mid-resolution (256+ px).

GAN ablation: Real-ESRGAN x4plus helps only when base signal is present. Scaling 64px→256px via GAN then detecting ≈ detecting native 256px (slight overfitting to hallucinations observed at 64px).
